<h3><strong>COMMITTEE OF SUPPLY DEBATES 2024<br>SPEECH BY SENIOR MINISTER OF STATE FOR MANPOWER AND DEFENCE ZAQY MOHAMAD</strong></h3>
<p></p>
<p><strong><em>Building Strong Foundations for Progress and Contribution</em></strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;Mr Chairman,</p>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>Introduction</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Our Malay/Muslim community has made significant progress in the last decade,
especially in education. As the community’s talent pool strengthens, it
provides strong foundations for our community to contribute back, and support
one together as a Community of Success.</p>
<p>&nbsp;2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
MENDAKI has played a key role in improving the education and professional
development outcomes of our community. We do so in three ways:</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; First, strengthening education as a pillar
for progress;</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Second, empowering our community to thrive
and contribute; and&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Third, grooming community leadership for the
future.</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Last year, MENDAKI engaged and supported close to 90,000 members of the
community through its various programmes and schemes. &nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;Strengthening Education as a Pillar for Progress</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;4.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
One of MENDAKI’s key pillars is to provide support for children and youth
at all stages of their education. To achieve this, one of our main approaches
is to mobilise talents and energies within the community.</p>
<p>&nbsp;5.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Mr Sharael Taha</strong> asked
about the progress of MENDAKI’s signature programmes like KelasMateMatika
(KMM) and the MENDAKI Tuition Programme (MTP).</p>
<p>&nbsp;6.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
I am happy to share that our <strong>KMM </strong>programme for pre-schoolers
saw a 74% increase in its enrolment between 2022 and 2023, with 1,700 children
and 1,500 parents enrolled in the programme in 2023.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Parental involvement has been an important
part of KMM, to ensure that parents are empowered and confident to support
their children’s learning.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; KMM’s scale up was possible with the help of
targeted outreach and referrals by partners such as Family Service Centres
(FSCs), Social Service Agencies (SSAs) and M3@Towns.</p>
<p>&nbsp;c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; More KMM facilitators have also come
on board, with their number tripling from about 50 in 2022 to more than
140 in 2023.</p>
<p>&nbsp;d.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; One KMM facilitator is Irfan Bin Ibrahim, a
Year 3 student at the National University of Singapore, who started volunteering
when he was serving his National Service in 2020. Volunteering shaped his
decision to take up a degree in psychology and pursue a career in the social
services sector, to continue contributing to the community.</p>
<p>&nbsp;7.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
The <strong>MTP</strong> continues to be the backbone of our educational
programmes, and enrolment for primary and secondary school students has
increased by 15% over the past 5 years, to over 9,000 students in 2023.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Today, MTP students are supported by 100 physical
and virtual centres.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; MENDAKI has also continually sought to improve
MTP offerings – such as by expanding its subjects offered to include Biology
and Additional Mathematics.</p>
<p>&nbsp;8.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Mr Fahmi Aliman</strong> asked
how we could encourage and inspire more people to step forward and serve,
especially those who have benefitted and done well. MENDAKI has many platforms
for the community to contribute their skills and passion to meet community
needs. An example is how our steadfast MTP tutors have played an integral
role in how MTP has grown from strength to strength.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; One such tutor is Anggreini Hamid,
who benefitted from MTP during her secondary school days. Now a schoolteacher
herself, she has been contributing back as an MTP tutor for the past 18
years. She adopts innovative techniques to make her classes more engaging
and effective for her students.</p>
<p>&nbsp;9.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
At the post-secondary and tertiary level, MENDAKI’s initiatives such as
the Youth Mentoring Office help students realise their potential. SPS Rahayu
will share more on these efforts in her speech.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>Empowering Our Community to Thrive and Contribute</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;10.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Beyond
a strong educational foundation, our Community of Success also needs a
workforce that can secure good jobs and meaningful careers. We want to
empower our workers to thrive so that they can grow our community and our
nation.</p>
<p>&nbsp;11.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Mr Fahmi Aliman</strong> and <strong>Mr Sharael Taha</strong> asked
about our plans to better support Malay/Muslim professionals and workers
in the future economy.<strong> </strong>MENDAKI continues to help our workforce
to develop their careers and professional networks, complementing national
efforts by our tripartite partners in Government, employers and the labour
movement.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<em>Support for Malay/Muslim Professionals</em>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;12.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; MENDAKI
initiated the <strong>Professional Networks</strong> to support Malay/Muslim
professionals in growing their networks, contribute back to the community,
exchange ideas and help younger workers enter growth industries.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To date, MENDAKI has formed nine Professional
Networks covering various industries in the Care, Green and Digital sectors,
and plan to form four more new networks in areas such as Healthcare and
Media this year.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; We now have more than 450 Malay/Muslim professionals
in these networks as of 2023, and MENDAKI will continue to encourage more
professionals to join and serve as mentors.</p>
<p>&nbsp;c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To <strong>Mr Sharael Taha’s</strong> question,
within both the professional Networks and beyond, we now have about 1,000
Malay/Muslim professionals contributing across MENDAKI’s initiatives, whether
it is serving as KMM facilitators, MTP tutors, youth mentors, or as speakers
and human libraries.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<em>Collaboration with Corporate Partners</em>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;13.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; In previous
COS debates, I shared about how corporate partners have partnered MENDAKI
to provide scholarships for students to enter different industries from
biomedical and finance, to aviation and technology.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; From this year, I am happy to announce that
Micron Semiconductor will come on board to provide scholarships over three
years to engineering students in polytechnics, as well as internships and
learning activities for secondary and tertiary students.</p>
<p>&nbsp;14.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; We appreciate
how companies also provide other forms of support, such as mentoring, to
our students.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; More than 50 MSD Pharma staff, such as data
scientists and those from the Artificial Intelligence and Cybersecurity
sectors, have signed up as mentors to 120 ITE students through the #amPowered@ITE
programme.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<em>Providing Support and Assurance to Workers</em>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;15.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; As our
economy and workforce evolves, MENDAKI will continue to support our Malay/Muslim
workforce in navigating career transitions and seizing future opportunities.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Through the M³ Focus Area 4 (FA4) on Employment
and Employability, MENDAKI employs a community-based strategy by collaborating
with M3 partners, including with the NTUC. More than 5,000 Malay/Muslim
workers have benefitted, including receiving support from MENDAKI’s Care
Advisors on their job search and career-related needs.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For women who wish to return to the workforce
after a career break, MENDAKI’s <strong>Women at Work (W@W)</strong> programme
equips them with the relevant skills and connecting them with job opportunities.</p>
<p>&nbsp;c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; In 2023, MENDAKI referred over 140 participants
from W@W to NTUC’s e2i for career coaching and job assistance, with close
to 60 successfully securing a job.</p>
<p>&nbsp;d.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; One of them is Ms Siti Raudah, who was looking
for a job to support her family and their three school-going children,
after her husband was retrenched. Ms Siti joined the W@W programme in September
last year, where she benefitted from support such as interview techniques,
and access to broader social and professional networks. Earlier this year,
Ms Siti found a job as an administrative executive in the oil and gas industry,
and shared that the programme has helped her to transit back into the workforce.</p>
<p>&nbsp;Chairman, in Malay, please.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>Membentuk Kepimpinan Masyarakat Masa Hadapan</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>16. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Strategi ketiga
kami adalah membangunkan pemimpin bukan sahaja untuk masyarakat kita, tetapi
juga negara.</p>
<p>&nbsp;17. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Cik Nadia Ahmad Samdin</strong> bertanya
tentang perkembangan terkini&nbsp; program Tunas.</p>
<p>&nbsp;18. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; MENDAKI melancarkan program <strong>Tunas bersama M³</strong> pada
tahun 2022 untuk memupuk barisan pemimpin seterusnya bagi pertubuhan Melayu/Islam
(MMO).</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Program 18 bulan ini bukan sahaja membekalkan
peserta dengan ilmu pengetahuan, kemahiran dan rangkaian kenalan agar dapat
menyumbang dan memimpin pertubuhan masing-masing. Malah kami mahu membantu
mereka untuk memainkan peranan kepimpinan lebih besar menjangkau masyarakat
Melayu/Islam pada masa hadapan.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Sebagai contoh, peserta dapat mempertingkatkan
kecekapan dalam bidang urus tadbir, usaha mengumpul dana, komunikasi, dan
pengurusan perubahan, serta berpeluang dibimbing oleh para pemimpin berpengalaman.</p>
<p>&nbsp;19. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Kohort pertama Tunas yang terdiri
daripada 13 peserta dari pelbagai MMO akan menamatkan program mereka pada
bulan April ini.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Para peserta dapat menggunakan ilmu dan kemahiran
yang diperolehi untuk menjalankan tujuh projek masyarakat yang sejajar
dengan bidang tumpuan M³.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Sebagai contoh, melalui Project Dream On, para
peserta Tunas bekerjasama dengan profesional daripada Rangkaian Karyawan&nbsp;
MENDAKI. Tujuan mereka ialah untuk memberi peluang kepada belia Melayu/Islam
berusia 15 hingga 17 tahun melalui latihan bekerja khusus kepada sektor-sektor
yang sedang membangun. Ini bagi membantu para belia meluaskan pengalaman
dan pola pemikiran&nbsp; mereka terhadap peluang pekerjaan masa hadapan.</p>
<p>&nbsp;c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Para peserta belia berkongsi bahawa pengalaman
ini telah membantu membina keyakinan, mendedahkan mereka kepada pelbagai
pengalaman dan meransang minat mereka, serta membantu untuk membentuk bidang
pengajian dan perancangan kerjaya mereka dengan lebih baik.</p>
<p>&nbsp;20. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Berdasarkan hasil positif program
ini, kami mengalu-alukan kohort kedua peserta Tunas pada bulan April nanti.
Kohort ini akan melibatkan para pemimpin daripada Rangkaian Karyawan MENDAKI.</p>
<p><strong>Conclusion</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;21. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Tuan Pengerusi, Mendaki akan
terus menyokong masyarakat Melayu/Islam kita untuk mencapai kecemerlangan
dan memperkasa masyarakat untuk menjadi warganegara yang aktif dan menyumbang.</p>
<p>&nbsp;22. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Namun, setiap individu harus
memainkan peranan untuk memastikan masyarakat kita dapat terus maju bersama.</p>
<p>&nbsp;23. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Saya amat mengalu-alukan para
pekerja Melayu/Islam kita untuk merebut pelbagai peluang bagi mempertingkatkan
kemahiran mereka melalui inisiatif yang diumumkan semasa Belanjawan 2024
baru-baru ini.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Misalnya, para pekerja pertengahan kerjaya
berusia 40 tahun dan ke atas yang ingin mempertingkatkan diri boleh&nbsp;
menyertai Program Peningkatan SkillsFuture. Program ini menawarkan <strong>tokokan kredit SkillsFuture sebanyak $4,000</strong> yang
boleh digunakan untuk kursus-kursus tertentu.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Para pekerja yang ingin mengambil diploma sepenuh
masa di politeknik, ITE dan institusi-institusi kesenian, juga dapat memanfaatkan
subsidi yang dipertingkatkan.</p>
<p>&nbsp;c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Bagi mengimbangi kehilangan pendapatan
bagi pekerja yang perlu menjalani latihan di luar waktu bekerja, Pemerintah
juga akan menyediakan <strong>Elaun Bulanan</strong> untuk <strong>Latihan Pertengahan Kerjaya SkillsFuture</strong>,
yang dihadkan pada $3,000, selama 24 bulan.</p>
<p>&nbsp;24. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Para lulusan ITE juga boleh memanfaatkan <strong>Anugerah Kemajuan ITE</strong> bernilai
$5,000 apabila mereka mendaftar ke program diploma, dan mendapat tambahan
$10,000 apabila tamat pengajian.</p>
<p>&nbsp;25. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Saya yakin bahawa sebagai satu
masyarakat, kita boleh memperkukuh budaya menyumbang. Dan, dengan kerjasama
Pemerintah dan badan-badan masyarakat yang lain, dapat mempertingkatkan
pencapaian masyarakat kita dalam pendidikan, pembangunan profesional serta
kepimpinan.</p>

<p><strong><u>English Translation</u></strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>Grooming Community Leadership for The Future</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;16.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Our third
strategy is to grow and develop leaders not only for our community, but
for our country as well.</p>
<p>&nbsp;17.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Ms Nadia Ahmad Samdin </strong>asked
for an update on the Tunas programme.</p>
<p>&nbsp;18.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; MENDAKI
started the <strong>Tunas Bersama M³ </strong>programme in 2022 to nurture
the next generation of leaders of Malay/Muslim Organisations (MMOs).</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; This 18-month programme not only equips emerging
leaders with the knowledge, skills and networks to contribute and lead
their own organisations, but also supports them in taking on larger leadership
roles in our wider community in the future.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For example, they develop competencies in the
areas of governance, fund raising, public communications, and change management,
as well as have the opportunity to be mentored by senior leaders.</p>
<p>&nbsp;19.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Our first
cohort of 13 Tunas participants from various MMOs will complete their programme
in April.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; They applied their learning to seven community
projects across the <strong>M³</strong> Focus Areas.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For example, through Project Dream On, Tunas
participants worked with professionals from MENDAKI’s Professional Networks
to provide Muslim youths aged 15 to 17 with sector-specific job shadowing
opportunities, to help them expand their horizons in terms of the future
job roles they could aspire to.</p>
<p>&nbsp;c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Youth participants shared that this experience
helped them grow as individuals, expand their range of interests, and better
shape their future field of study and career plans.</p>
<p>&nbsp;20.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Building
on the positive outcomes of this programme, we will welcome our second
batch of Tunas participants in April this year, which will also include
leaders from MENDAKI’s Professional Networks.</p>
<p><strong>Conclusion</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>21.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Mr Chairman, MENDAKI will continue to support our Malay/Muslim community
in reaching greater heights and empower the community to be active, contributing
citizens.</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>22.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
For our community to continue progressing together, this has to be a collective
endeavour where everyone has a part to play.</p>
<p>&nbsp;23.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; On this
note, I strongly encourage our Malay/Muslim workers to take up the opportunities
to upskill and reskill, that were recently announced during Budget 2024.</p>
<p>&nbsp;a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Mid-career workers aged 40 and
above looking at reskilling can consider the <strong>SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme</strong>,
which includes a <strong>$4,000 SkillsFuture Credit top-up</strong> that
can be used for selected courses.</p>
<p>&nbsp;b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; There is also the <strong>Mid-Career Enhanced Subsidy</strong> for
workers to take on another full-time diploma at the Polytechnics, ITE and
Arts Institutions.</p>
<p>c.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To offset income loss for workers who
have to take time off work for training, the Government will also provide
a monthly SkillsFuture Mid-Career Training Allowance, capped at $3,000,
for up to 24 months.&nbsp;</p>
<p>24.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Our younger
ITE graduates can also benefit through the <strong>ITE Progression Award</strong> of
$5,000 upon enrolment and another $10,000 upon completion, when they upgrade
through a diploma qualification.</p>
<p>25.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; I am confident
that as a community, we can grow a culture of contribution, and in partnership
with the Government and other partners, do much more together to support
efforts in education, professional development, and community leadership.&nbsp;</p>
